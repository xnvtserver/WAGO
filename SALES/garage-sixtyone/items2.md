# **Sales Call Script — Software, Web Development & Booking System**

---

## **1. Opening**

**Sales Rep:**
“Hi, is this *[Client Name]*?
This is *[Your Name]* from *Your Company Name*.
Thanks for showing interest in website & booking application development.
Is this a good time to talk for 3–5 minutes?”

---

## **2. Discovery Questions (Pain Points)**

**Sales Rep:**
“To understand your requirement fully, I have a few quick questions.”

### **Business Need**

* “Are you looking for a website, booking system, or both?”
* “Do you need online payments?”
* “Are you currently using any system or doing it manually?”

### **Current Pain**

* “What challenges you face right now — customers not finding you online, booking confusion, or lack of professionalism?”
* “Any issues with your old website, if you had one?”

### **Future Goals**

* “How many customers do you expect daily/weekly?”
* “Do you plan to expand into multiple branches later?”

---

## **3. Positioning / Pitch**

**Sales Rep:**
“Perfect, based on what you shared, here’s what we can solve:”

* Build a clean, branded website for your shop
* Online booking for servicing
* Automatic reminders (SMS/WhatsApp)
* Dashboard for viewing upcoming bookings
* Hosting & domain handled by us
* Maintenance, security & uptime managed by us

---

## **4. Social Proof**

“We’ve worked with auto workshops, salons, and clinics — and they increased customer flow by 30–50% because of proper online presence.”

---

## **5. Pricing Close**

“Our pricing starts at **₹X**, including website + booking system + hosting setup.
If you’re okay, I can send the proposal and schedule a demo.”

---

## **6. Objection Handling**

**“Cost is high.”**
→ “I understand. But this replaces manual registers, WhatsApp confusion, and gives a professional image. It brings more customers.”

**“Let me think.”**
→ “Sure. Can I send you a detailed proposal along with example designs?”

**“We don’t need an advanced system.”**
→ “We can start small and upgrade as your business grows.”

---

## **7. Closing**

“Great, I’ll send the proposal. When shall we connect next — today evening or tomorrow morning?”
